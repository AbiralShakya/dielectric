> "Heuristics are rules of thumb with no guarantees. We use proven algorithms - Voronoi diagrams use Fortune's O(n log n) algorithm that's mathematically proven optimal for spatial partitioning. MST uses Kruskal's algorithm that's optimal for minimum total distance. These have mathematical guarantees, not just 'good enough' solutions."

### **"Why computational geometry?"**
> "Computational geometry provides rigorous mathematical structures that capture spatial relationships. Voronoi diagrams create optimal spatial partitions, MST finds optimal connections, Gaussian models use actual thermal physics. These are interpretable - engineers can verify our calculations independently, unlike black-box AI."

### **"How do you handle NP-hard placement?"**
> "PCB placement is NP-hard, so we use heuristics for full optimization. But we use proven, optimal algorithms for spatial analysis - Voronoi, MST, Convex Hull - with mathematical guarantees. Then we use these optimal geometric insights to guide our placement heuristics intelligently. It's optimal algorithms where possible, intelligent heuristics where necessary."

### **"What makes this explainable?"**
> "Our geometric metrics are interpretable. Voronoi variance = 0.15 means uniform distribution - engineers can verify this calculation. MST length = 125mm means optimal trace routing estimate - engineers can check our edge list. Thermal hotspots are identified using Gaussian physics - engineers can validate our model. This is verifiable mathematics, not black-box AI."

### **"How do you know these algorithms work?"**
> "All our algorithms are from peer-reviewed research. Fortune 1987 proved Voronoi diagrams are optimal for spatial partitioning. Kruskal 1956 proved MST is optimal for minimum total distance. Holman 2010 provides the thermal physics equations. These are battle-tested algorithms used in GIS, robotics, graphics - not experimental methods."
